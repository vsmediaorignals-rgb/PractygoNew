How to Rank #1 on Google Maps as a Doctor in India

Your Google Business Profile is your most powerful local SEO asset. Here's the complete optimisation checklist we use for every new healthcare client — the same steps that helped Dr. Priya Menon rank #1 in Bengaluru in 6 weeks.

✍️ Practygo Team
📅 Feb 28, 2025
⏱️ 6 min read

When a patient in your city types "cardiologist near me" or "best skin doctor in [your area]" into Google, they see three things before any website link: a map and three clinic listings. This is the Google Local Pack — the most valuable piece of real estate in local search, and it's driven entirely by your Google Business Profile (GBP).

Getting into that top-3 isn't magic. It follows a clear algorithm with well-understood ranking signals. Here's the complete playbook we use at Practygo.

Step 1: Claim and Verify Your Profile

This sounds obvious but you'd be surprised — a significant percentage of Indian clinic GBP listings are either unclaimed, claimed by a former employee, or duplicated. Go to business.google.com, search for your clinic, and claim ownership. Google will verify via postcard, phone call, or video verification.

⚠️ Watch out for duplicates. If someone has already created a listing for your clinic, you'll need to request ownership. Google allows this through a standard request form. Duplicate listings split your reviews and hurt ranking — always merge or remove them.

Step 2: Complete Every Single Field

Google's algorithm rewards completeness. Most clinics fill in only the basics — name, address, phone. Here's the full checklist:

  • Business name (exactly as it appears on your clinic signage — no keyword stuffing)
  • Primary category (e.g. "Cardiologist", "Dermatologist") — be specific, not just "Doctor"
  • Secondary categories (e.g. "Medical Clinic", "Heart Hospital")
  • Full address with pin code — must exactly match your website and Practo listing
  • Service area (if you do home visits or teleconsultation)
  • Phone number — use a local landline or mobile, not a toll-free number
  • Website URL — your own clinic website, not just Practo
  • Opening hours including Saturday/Sunday and holiday hours
  • Appointment booking link
  • Attributes (e.g. "Accepts new patients", "Online care", "Wheelchair accessible")
  • Business description (750 characters — use your specialty, location, and patient promise)

Step 3: Upload Real, High-Quality Photos

Clinics with 10+ photos receive significantly more clicks and direction requests than those with none. Upload photos of the following, all taken in good natural or studio lighting:

  • Clinic exterior (so patients can identify it on arrival)
  • Reception and waiting area
  • Consultation room
  • Doctor in professional setting (not a stock photo)
  • Any specialist equipment or facilities

Name your photo files descriptively before uploading — e.g. dr-sharma-cardiology-clinic-delhi.jpg rather than IMG_4521.jpg. This adds minor but real SEO value.

Step 4: Build a Review Strategy (Not a Fake Review Strategy)

Reviews are Google Maps' single biggest ranking factor. The quantity, recency, and your response rate all matter. Here's how to build reviews ethically:

Ask at the right moment

The best time to ask a patient for a review is immediately after a positive interaction — when they thank you at the end of a consultation. Have your receptionist hand them a small card with a QR code linking to your Google review page.

Make it effortless

Create a short review link using Google's Place ID tool: g.page/[your-business-name]/review. Put this link in your post-visit WhatsApp follow-up message.

Respond to every review

Google's algorithm values your response rate. Respond to all reviews within 48 hours — even negative ones. For positives, a simple personalised thank-you is enough. For negatives, see our guide on how to handle 1-star reviews.

📊 Practygo benchmark: Clinics that actively request reviews and respond to 100% of them rank an average of 2.3 positions higher than clinics that don't, within the same geographic area and specialty.

Step 5: Post Weekly Google Business Updates

Most doctors don't know this exists: Google Business Profiles have a Posts feature — similar to a mini social media feed that appears directly on your Maps listing. Posting weekly (appointment availability, health tips, clinic updates, seasonal health advice) signals to Google that your business is active. Active listings rank higher.

Step 6: Build Local Citations

A citation is any online mention of your clinic's Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP). Google cross-references these to confirm your business is real and where you say it is. Build citations on:

  • Practo (essential for Indian healthcare)
  • JustDial
  • Sulekha
  • 1mg
  • Lybrate
  • IndiaMart (for speciality clinics)
  • Local hospital websites you're affiliated with

Consistency is critical: Your clinic name, address, and phone must be identical across every platform — even small variations like "St." vs "Street" create conflicting signals.

Step 7: Understand Proximity — The Factor You Can't Change

Google Maps heavily weights how close the searcher is to your clinic. If someone is 15 km away, you may not appear in their Local Pack even if you've done everything right. This is why your strategy should also include neighbourhood-specific keywords on your website (e.g. "cardiologist in Koregaon Park, Pune") to capture patients searching with location qualifiers rather than "near me."
